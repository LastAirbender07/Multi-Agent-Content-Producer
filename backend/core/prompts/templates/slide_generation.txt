Create {target_slides} slides (aim for 12-14). Each slide MUST be packed with real, specific information. People stop and share slides that teach them something concrete.

SLIDE TYPES AND CONTENT REQUIREMENTS:

- hook     : First slide only. A 6-9 word headline that creates urgent curiosity or shock. No fluff.

- content  : Main insight slide. Title (max 8 words) + body (30-55 words of specific insight, full sentences).
             MUST include bullets: 3-5 numbered points, each 10-20 words, giving concrete facts, steps, or comparisons.
             These bullets ARE the substance — make them specific and information-dense.

- stat     : One shocking number (stat_value) + descriptive label (stat_label, 5-12 words).
             Title: context sentence (8-12 words explaining why this stat matters).
             Body: 20-35 words explaining the implication or cause.
             chart_type: choose the BEST type for the data (see chart type guide below).
             chart_data: structured data object matching the chart type format.
             Leave bullets as empty [].

- quote    : A direct quote or punchy pull-quote from your research. Keep title under 35 words.
             Body: 10-20 words attributing the quote (who said it, when/where).
             bullets: 2-3 key takeaways this quote illustrates (10-15 words each). These appear as "Key Insights" below the quote.

- engage   : One mid-carousel engagement prompt. Appears around slide 5-7 to catch viewers before they drop off.
             Title: direct, warm call to follow/like/save (10-16 words). E.g. "If this surprised you, follow — we break down research like this every week."
             Body: 1-2 sentences reinforcing why following is worth it (specific, not generic).
             Leave bullets, stat fields, and chart fields empty.

- cta      : Exactly 2 CTA slides total.
             Early CTA (~position 6, right after the engage): brief and punchy — aimed at converting
             readers who've just absorbed the data. Title: 8-12 words, high energy. Body: 1 sentence.
             Final CTA (last slide): comprehensive close — save/share/comment ask + what they'll gain.
             Title: action-oriented (10-16 words). Body: 1-2 sentences reinforcing the value.

CHART TYPE GUIDE (for stat slides — pick based on data shape):
- "bar"     → comparing named categories side-by-side (most common)
- "column"  → same but left-to-right order is chronological or sequential
- "donut"   → parts of a whole where values are proportions (use when values sum to ~100%)
- "line"    → a metric changing over years/time periods
- "radar"   → comparing the same set of attributes across 2 subjects
- "funnel"  → stages in a process where each value is a subset of the previous

bar or column: {{"labels": ["A", "B", "C"], "values": [73, 45, 12]}}
donut:         {{"labels": ["Group A", "Group B", "Group C"], "values": [73, 18, 9]}}

funnel:        {{"labels": ["Stage1","Stage2","Stage3"], "values": [100, 72, 8]}}
